I’m a research assistant professor at CIERA at Northwestern, where I work on the lives and deaths of massive stars—their variability, mass loss, explosions, and compact remnants. I run optical and infrared time-domain programs with telescopes in Arizona, Hawaii, and Chile to find and follow these events in the local Universe.

I’m interested in supernovae and their progenitors, gravitational wave astronomy, and fast radio bursts. I work on explosive transients and their progenitor sources across the electromagnetic spectrum, including through the Young Supernova Experiment, the TROVE Collaboration, and the F4 Collaboration. I was a CIERA postdoc from 2020–2023 and joined the faculty in 2023.
